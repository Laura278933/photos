The Big Rock Pool Challenge is a UK-wide citizen science initiative run by the Rock Pool Project, bringing people together to explore and record life on UK rocky shores. Participants take part in monthly BioBlitz Battle games, recording coastal marine life at designated monitoring hubs using the iNaturalist platform and the BioBlitz Battle app.

Records are verified through community identifications, allowing observations to reach research-grade status. Every record added helps build a growing picture of coastal biodiversity around the UK.

The Big Rock Pool Challenge is run by The Rock Pool Project, in partnership with the Marine Biological Association, and is funded by the ScottishPower Foundation and the National Lottery Heritage Fund.

Big Rock Pool Challenge Biodiversity Report - Q1 2026

This report brings together all observations recorded through the Big Rock Pool Challenge up to March 2026, with a closer look at activity during Quarter 1 of 2026 (January–March).

All project data

The Big Rock Pool Challenge commenced in October 2024 at hubs located at Castle Beach, Falmouth and Mount Batten, Plymouth. In October 2025, seven new hubs joined the Big Rock Pool Challenge:

  • Shoeburyness, Southend-on-Sea
  • Kilve Beach, Somerset
  • Beadnell Haven, Northumberland
  • East Sands, St Andrews
  • Lee-on-the-Solent, Hampshire
  • Menai Bridge, Bangor
  • Ovingdean, Brighton

Data presented in this section of the report reflect all Big Rock Pool Challenge records from the start of the project in October 2024, until the end of Quarter 1 (end of March) 2026.

To view data from Quarter 1 of 2026 only, please see the second half of this report, or click here.

Overall Project Summary

23,379 observations · 711 contributors · 1,391 species

Species are grouped into higher-level “iconic taxa” categories based on their taxonomic ancestry. This grouping simplifies complex species-level data into broader biological categories (e.g. molluscs, crustaceans, algae), allowing clearer interpretation of community structure across sites and time periods.

The Big Rock Pool Challenge is powered by people. Every observation and identification is a contribution to a shared understanding of coastal biodiversity.

Some participants frequently attend events and submit records, while others help out by identifying species through verification on iNaturalist- all contributions are equally valuable in building the dataset.

For all project data until end of March 2026, records were submitted by 711 recorders, and verified to research grade by 1370 identifiers.

Each hub represents a local coastal site where volunteers return to record marine life over time. These locations form the backbone of the Big Rock Pool Challenge network.

Because each hub has been running for different lengths of time, and is explored by different groups of people, the number of records varies, but every contribution adds valuable information about rock pool communities at that site.

During BioBlitz Battle games, all species recorded earn a score. These scores reflect how often this species is recorded in the overall Big Rock Pool Challenge dataset. The higher scoring species are those that are recorded less often within the project and may represent more unusual or less commonly observed species.
